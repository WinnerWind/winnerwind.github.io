Inspired by Morg's Loop
When approaching language learning, it's easy to be intimidated or lead down the wrong path. This loop, which is a generalised implementation of Morg's Loop for Japanese hopes to make language learning easy and fun.
This is a guideline and not a syllabus. There is no syllabus to being fluent in any language.
Also note that I am mostly a monolingual with some knowledge in Hindi, Bengali and Japanese. I am in no way qualified to give such generalised advice. This is just what has worked best for me to get my language proficiency to where I am now.

First Steps

The decision to start learning is the strongest one. However, it is also important to do it efficiently.
If the language you are learning has its own script, it is worth learning that script. Don't focus on perfection, just have a decent idea of what each letter sounds like.
You will need to learn some basic grammar before you can start the soul of the loop. Although in theory you could skip this step, it will cause you to have many doubts and questions. Optionally pick up a grammar deck or a vocabulary deck. Check out Anki for this. It is a free, open source flashcards app that has helped me memorise thousands of words in mere months.

The Input Loop.

According to the Input Hypothesis1, understanding what you read is more important than the how. In short, why worry about why certain grammar is a certain way, when you already understand what you are reading? Of course, this is impossible to do so in the early steps, but just by understanding what certain nouns mean, you can draw a mental relationship between them.
1: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Input_hypothesis
